## What Happened

According to supply chain sources and regulatory filings spotted this week, Meta's Quest 4 headset is shaping up to be a major upgrade over the Quest 3. The most significant change: a switch to micro-OLED display technology, matching what Apple uses in the Vision Pro.

The leaks suggest Meta is targeting a late 2025 release, with production ramping up in Q3. This aligns with Meta's typical two-year cadence between major Quest releases.

## Key Details

- **Display**: Dual micro-OLED panels with 2400x2400 resolution per eye (up from 2064x2208 in Quest 3)
- **Optics**: Advanced pancake lenses with wider field of view (120 degrees vs 110 degrees)
- **Processor**: Qualcomm Snapdragon XR3 Gen 2 chip (not yet announced)
- **Price**: Expected $599-$699 range
- **Release**: Q4 2025 (November/December timeframe)

## Why It Matters

### For Consumers

Micro-OLED displays would eliminate the screen door effect that still plagues current VR headsets, including the Quest 3. Colors will be more vibrant, blacks truly black, and text dramatically sharper. This matters enormously for productivity use cases Meta has been pushing with Quest for Business.

### For the Industry

Meta moving to micro-OLED puts serious pressure on competitors. Sony's PlayStation VR2 uses OLED, and Apple's Vision Pro set the premium standard with micro-OLED. If Meta can deliver this technology at $600-700, it forces everyone else to either match the quality or compete purely on price.

### For Investors

This represents Meta's continued massive investment in Reality Labs, which has burned through over $40 billion since 2020. The Quest 4 needs to be a hit to justify Zuckerberg's metaverse bet. Early VR adopters will scrutinize whether this justifies upgrading from Quest 3.

## The Backstory

Meta launched Quest 3 in October 2023 at $499, positioning it as the mainstream mixed reality headset. Quest 3 sold well initially but hasn't reached the mass market penetration Meta hoped for. Meanwhile, Apple entered the space with Vision Pro at $3,499, setting a new bar for display quality and passthrough AR.

The pressure is on Meta to deliver Vision Pro-like visual quality at a fraction of the price. Micro-OLED displays have gotten cheaper since Apple's launch, making this timing possible.

## What's Next

**Timeline:**
- **Q2 2025**: Official announcement expected at Meta Connect or dedicated event
- **Q3 2025**: Production ramp-up and developer kit distribution
- **Q4 2025**: Consumer launch, likely November to catch holiday season

Watch for Qualcomm to announce the Snapdragon XR3 Gen 2 chip in the coming months - that will effectively confirm Quest 4's existence and capabilities.

## Our Take

This leak pattern feels deliberate. Meta benefits from managing expectations before Apple potentially announces Vision Pro 2 at WWDC in June. By getting "Quest 4 with micro-OLED" into the conversation now, Meta primes consumers to wait for their headset rather than buying Vision Pro.

The $600-700 price point is aggressive but achievable. Meta sells hardware at cost or slight loss, subsidized by the Meta Store's 30% cut and data collection value. Sony's micro-OLED panels have dropped 40% in price since 2023.

The real question: will visual upgrades alone drive Quest 3 owners to upgrade? Meta needs compelling software - think Horizon Worlds 2.0 or exclusive AAA VR titles - to make this a must-buy rather than a nice-to-have.

## The Bottom Line

Meta Quest 4's leaked specs suggest a genuine generational leap in visual quality, bringing standalone VR closer to premium tethered experiences. If Meta delivers micro-OLED at $699, it could finally crack the mainstream VR market. The timing forces both consumers and competitors to recalibrate expectations for what mid-range VR can deliver in 2025.
