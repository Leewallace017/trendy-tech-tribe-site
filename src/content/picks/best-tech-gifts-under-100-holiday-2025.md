## Buying Guide

### How We Selected These Gifts

We focused on tech gifts that:
- **Solve real problems** - Not novelty gadgets that sit unused
- **Work out of the box** - Minimal setup friction
- **Have longevity** - Won't feel outdated in 6 months
- **Fit diverse users** - Tech enthusiasts and skeptics alike
- **Stay within budget** - Typically available under $100, especially during Black Friday sales

### What to Avoid

**Cheap knockoffs** - That $15 "wireless earbud" on Amazon won't last a month. Stick to known brands.

**Overly specialized gadgets** - A smart egg tray or Bluetooth-enabled hairbrush sounds fun but rarely gets used.

**Last-gen flagship tech** - A 2-year-old "premium" device is often worse than a current mid-range option.

**Platform-locked gifts** - Avoid gifts that only work within Apple/Android ecosystem unless you're certain of recipient's preference.

### Black Friday & Cyber Monday Tips

**Expected discounts for these categories:**
- Smart speakers: 30-50% off (Echo Dot often hits $25-30)
- Power banks: 20-30% off
- Streaming devices: 30-40% off (Roku Stick 4K often $35-40)
- Bluetooth trackers: 25-35% off
- Mice/keyboards: 15-25% off

**Strategy:**
1. Add items to cart now
2. Set up price tracking alerts (CamelCamelCamel, Honey)
3. Check prices on Black Friday (Nov 29)
4. Buy on Cyber Monday (Dec 2) if Black Friday sold out

*Note: Check Amazon for current pricing on all recommendations—holiday sales make these items even better values.*

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the best tech gift for someone who "has everything"?

The Tile Mate 4-pack. Even tech enthusiasts lose things. A 4-pack means they can tag items they didn't even realize they needed to track (TV remote, garage door opener, bike lock key).

### Are these gifts good for non-tech-savvy recipients?

Absolutely. We specifically chose items with simple setup and intuitive interfaces. The Echo Dot and Roku Stick require virtually zero technical knowledge—plug in, follow on-screen prompts, done.

### Should I buy extended warranties from Amazon?

For items under $100, usually no. These products are generally reliable, and the warranty cost approaches 20-30% of the purchase price. Exception: If gifting to someone rough on electronics, AppleCare+ or SquareTrade might be worth it.

### What if the recipient already has one of these?

Most of these products are useful in multiples:
- Echo Dots: Useful in multiple rooms
- Power banks: One for car, one for bag
- Tile trackers: Always need more
- Roku: Guest room, basement TV

Only the MX Master 3S wouldn't make sense as a duplicate.
