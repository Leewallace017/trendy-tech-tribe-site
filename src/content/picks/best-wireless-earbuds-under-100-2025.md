## What to Look For

Shopping for wireless earbuds under $100 requires trade-offs, but not as many as you'd think. Here's what actually matters:

1. **Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)** - No longer premium-only; available in budget options
2. **Battery Life** - Look for 8+ hours per charge, 24+ hours with case
3. **Sound Quality** - Balanced profile matters more than gimmicky "bass boost"
4. **Codec Support** - AAC minimum, LDAC/aptX nice to have
5. **Comfort & Fit** - Multiple ear tip sizes essential
6. **Water Resistance** - IPX4 minimum for workout use

## Buying Guide

### How We Tested

We evaluated 15+ wireless earbuds under $100 based on:
- **Sound quality**: Balanced frequency response, detail retrieval, soundstage
- **ANC effectiveness**: dB reduction in real-world environments (subway, cafe, airplane)
- **Battery life**: Actual playback time vs. claimed specs
- **Codec support**: AAC, LDAC, aptX tested with various source devices
- **Comfort**: Extended wear testing (2+ hour sessions)
- **Build quality**: Materials, hinge durability, water resistance

### What We Avoided

**Red flags in budget earbuds:**
- Unknown brands with fake reviews (99% 5-stars is suspicious)
- No codec information listed (likely using basic SBC only)
- Unrealistic battery claims (50 hours per earbud is physically impossible)
- No water resistance rating (will fail quickly from sweat/rain)
- Poor app support or abandoned firmware

### Price Tiers Explained

**Under $50**:
- Basic Bluetooth earbuds
- No ANC, limited battery life (5-6 hours)
- Sound quality varies wildly
- Worth it for: Gym/workout earbuds you don't mind destroying

**$50-80 (Sweet spot)**:
- ANC available from brands like Anker, Soundcore
- 8-10 hour battery life standard
- Good sound quality if you choose right model
- Best value tier overall

**$80-100 (Premium budget)**:
- Flagship features (LDAC, adaptive ANC, multipoint)
- Rivaling $150+ models from big brands
- Worth it for: Those who want near-flagship performance on budget

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are wireless earbuds under $100 worth it vs. cheap wired earbuds?

Absolutely, but with caveats. A $15 pair of wired earbuds might sound better than $50 wireless, but the convenience of wireless is significant. In the $80-100 range, wireless earbuds sound as good or better than wired equivalents.

### Do budget earbuds have good ANC?

Yes! The ANC revolution has reached budget earbuds. The Soundcore Liberty 4 NC and Space A40 both deliver ANC that rivals Sony and Bose models costing $200+. The main difference: premium models have better microphones for calls and slightly more refined tuning.

### What's the difference between these and AirPods?

**AirPods Pro 2** ($249):
- Better call quality in noisy environments
- Spatial audio with head tracking
- Premium build quality and brand cache
- Seamless Apple ecosystem integration

**Budget picks ($50-100)**:
- 80% of the sound quality
- Comparable or better battery life
- Strong ANC (on higher-end budget models)
- No spatial audio, less refined app experience

For most users, budget options deliver 90% of the experience at 40% of the cost.

### Can you use these for working out?

Yes, if they have IPX4 or higher rating. The JBL Live Pro 2 (IP55) are especially good for workouts due to better sweat/water resistance. Avoid Beats Flex for intense workouts (neckband design uncomfortable when sweaty).
