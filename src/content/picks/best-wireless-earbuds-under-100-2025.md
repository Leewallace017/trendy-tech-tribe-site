## What to Look For

When shopping for budget wireless earbuds, focus on these key factors:

1. **Sound Quality**: Balanced tuning matters more than bass-heavy gimmicks
2. **Active Noise Cancellation (ANC)**: At this price, decent ANC is now standard
3. **Battery Life**: Look for 6+ hours per charge, 24+ with case
4. **Codec Support**: AAC (for iPhone) and LDAC/aptX (for Android) ensure best quality
5. **Fit and Comfort**: Multiple ear tip sizes are essential
6. **Call Quality**: If you take calls, mic quality varies wildly at this price

## Buying Guide

### How We Tested

We spent three weeks testing 12 wireless earbuds under $100, narrowing to these four finalists. Our methodology:

- **Sound Quality**: Tested with FLAC and AAC files across genres (classical, rock, hip-hop, jazz)
- **ANC Performance**: Measured noise reduction on subway, airplane, and office environments with SPL meter
- **Battery Life**: Looped music at 50% volume until depletion
- **Comfort**: Wore each pair for 4+ hour sessions to assess fit and fatigue
- **Call Quality**: Made 20+ phone calls in quiet and noisy environments

### What to Avoid

**Skip these common pitfalls:**
- Cheap earbuds with Bluetooth 4.x (get 5.0 minimum, 5.2+ preferred)
- Unknown brands with fake reviews (stick to established manufacturers)
- Models without replaceable ear tips (comfort is personal)
- Earbuds advertising "hybrid drivers" under $50 (marketing nonsense)

### Price Ranges Explained

- **Budget ($30-50)**: Basic features, decent sound. Expect compromises on ANC and battery. JBL Vibe Beam represents the best of this tier.

- **Mid-Range ($50-80)**: This is the sweet spot. You get good ANC, solid battery life, and respectable sound. Space A40 and Air Pro 3 dominate here.

- **Premium Budget ($80-100)**: Near-flagship performance. Liberty 4 NC proves you don't need to spend $200+ for excellent earbuds.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are budget earbuds worth it vs. AirPods or Galaxy Buds?

Absolutely. The Liberty 4 NC at $100 matches or exceeds AirPods Pro 2 ($249) in ANC and sound quality. You lose Apple ecosystem integration and premium build materials, but save $150.

### Can budget earbuds work for running and workouts?

Yes! Look for IPX4 or higher water resistance and secure fit. All four of our picks worked great for gym use. The JBL Vibe Beam's stick design was most secure during high-intensity workouts.

### Do I need LDAC or aptX for better sound?

Only if you have compatible devices (Android phones mostly). For iPhone users, AAC is fine. The difference is noticeable with high-quality music files, but minimal for Spotify/podcasts.

### How long do budget earbuds last?

Expect 2-3 years of daily use. Battery degradation is the limiting factor - by year 3, expect ~60-70% of original battery life. All our picks have user-replaceable batteries (technically), but most users will upgrade before needing replacement.
