## What to Look For

When shopping for tech toys for your cat, these factors matter most:

1.  **Interactivity**: Does the toy respond to your cat's touch or movement? Static toys get boring fast.
2.  **Variety**: Toys that offer multiple modes (laser, feather, sound) keep cats engaged longer.
3.  **Durability**: Cats have claws. Look for sturdy materials and protected electronics.
4.  **Power Source**: USB rechargeable toys save you money on batteries in the long run.

## Buying Guide

### Safety First
Always supervise your cat with new toys, especially those with strings or small parts. Even "automatic" toys should be used when you're around to ensure safety.

### Rotation is Key
Cats get bored. Don't leave all these toys out 24/7. Rotate them every few days to keep the novelty alive.

### The "Hunt" Cycle
To really satisfy your cat, let them "catch" the toy (especially the bird or mouse) and then feed them a treat. This completes the natural "Hunt-Catch-Kill-Eat" cycle and leaves them satisfied.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are laser toys bad for cats?
They can be frustrating if the cat never "catches" anything. Always finish a laser session by letting them catch a physical toy or giving them a treat.

### How long do the batteries last?
For the USB rechargeable toys (Bird, Ball, Wand), a single charge typically lasts 1-2 hours of continuous play, which translates to several days of intermittent use.

### Can these toys replace playtime with me?
No! These are great for when you're busy, but nothing replaces direct bonding time with a wand toy and your cat.
