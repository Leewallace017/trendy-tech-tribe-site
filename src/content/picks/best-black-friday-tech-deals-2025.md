**Fake Discounts**: Some retailers inflate "original" prices to make discounts look bigger. Check price history tools before assuming you're getting a deal.

**Unnecessary Features**: Don't pay extra for features you won't use. A basic Echo Dot does 90% of what expensive smart speakers do for most users.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are Black Friday tech deals actually better than other times of year?

For specific categories like Amazon devices, smart home products, and TVs, Black Friday typically offers the best prices of the year. However, some tech products see comparable or better deals during Amazon Prime Day (July) or back-to-school sales (August). The advantage of Black Friday is the breadth of deals across categories.

### Should I wait until Cyber Monday for better deals?

Black Friday and Cyber Monday deals are largely identical in 2025, with most retailers running continuous sales from Thanksgiving through the following Monday. The main difference is that Black Friday focuses on in-store doorbuster deals, while Cyber Monday emphasizes online-exclusive offers. For the products in this guide, prices are unlikely to change significantly between Friday and Monday.

### How can I tell if a Black Friday deal is genuine?

Use price tracking tools like CamelCamelCamel or Keepa to see historical pricing. A genuine deal should be at or near the lowest price the product has been in the past 6-12 months. Be skeptical of "original price" claims—focus on the actual sale price compared to recent typical pricing.

### Will these products be in stock throughout Black Friday weekend?

Popular items like Echo Dots and Fire TV Sticks often sell out and restock multiple times during Black Friday weekend. If something shows as out of stock, check back every few hours. Less popular items typically remain available throughout the sale period.
