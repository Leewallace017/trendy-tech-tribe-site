## What Happened

Nvidia reports fiscal Q4 2024 earnings this Wednesday, January 29th, after market close. Wall Street expects another blowout quarter as hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) continue ordering Hopper H100 and new H200 GPUs at unprecedented rates.

Consensus estimates project $20.4 billion in revenue (up 231% year-over-year) and earnings per share of $4.59 (up 417% YoY). But the real story is 2025 guidance: can Nvidia sustain triple-digit growth as Blackwell chips ramp production?

## Key Details

- **Report Date**: Wednesday, January 29, 2025, ~4:15 PM ET
- **Revenue Estimate**: $20.4B (range: $19.8B - $21.2B)
- **EPS Estimate**: $4.59 (range: $4.45 - $4.75)
- **Gross Margin Forecast**: 75.5% (up from 74.0% in Q3)
- **Key Wildcard**: Q1 2025 guidance (Blackwell ramp implications)

## Why It Matters

### For Tech Investors

Nvidia has added over $2 trillion in market cap since ChatGPT launched in November 2022. At $800/share, it trades at 35x forward earnings - not cheap, but justified if AI infrastructure spending continues accelerating. A guidance miss could trigger 20%+ selloff; a beat could push the stock toward $1,000.

### For the AI Industry

Nvidia's results effectively report the health of the entire AI buildout. Strong guidance confirms Microsoft, Google, and others are still in land-grab mode for AI compute. Weak guidance suggests AI spending is moderating or customers are waiting for next-gen Blackwell chips.

### For the Broader Market

Nvidia represents 6.5% of the S&P 500's market cap. When Nvidia moves 10%, the S&P 500 moves ~0.65% in the same direction. Tech funds are wildly overweight NVDA - a massive beat or miss will ripple across the entire market Thursday morning.

## The Backstory

Nvidia's earnings have become quarterly spectacles. In November 2024, the company reported Q3 revenue of $18.1B, beating estimates by $1B. The stock initially surged 8% after-hours, then gave back gains as management cautioned about Blackwell supply constraints.

Since then, two key developments: 1) TSMC announced Blackwell production is ahead of schedule, 2) Multiple hyperscalers confirmed AI CapEx budgets are increasing for 2025, not decreasing. This sets up potential for a guidance beat.

The bear case rests on inventory concerns. Channel checks suggest some H100 chips are sitting unused as customers wait for more powerful H200 and Blackwell options. If true, Nvidia may need to cut prices or slow production - both would pressure margins.

## Expert Reactions

**Hans Mosesmann** (Rosenblatt Securities, price target $1,000): "Nvidia's H200 is sold out for all of 2025. Blackwell demand exceeds supply by 3x. This scarcity pricing dynamic continues through at least mid-2026."

**Jim Cramer** (CNBC) warned investors: "Don't chase Nvidia into earnings. The bar is stratospheric. Unless Jensen Huang announces Blackwell is shipping 6 months early with 50% better performance, the stock could sell off on any guidance that's merely 'great' instead of 'perfect.'"

## Our Take

The setup favors a modest beat-and-raise. Supply chain intelligence suggests Blackwell yields improved dramatically in December. TSMC's CoWoS advanced packaging capacity (the bottleneck for high-end AI chips) is expanding faster than expected.

The bigger question: Has AI infrastructure spending peaked? Microsoft, Meta, and Google all telegraphed continued heavy CapEx in recent calls. Amazon's CapEx guidance was lighter than expected, but AWS revenue growth accelerated. The data points to sustained, not peaking, demand.

Positioning: Nvidia likely trades down into earnings (typical pattern), then rallies post-report if guidance beats. Options traders are pricing a ~8% move either direction - that feels about right. The risk is that excellence is now expected, and merely "great" results disappoint.

The Blackwell commentary will matter more than Q4 numbers. If Jensen Huang confirms Blackwell is ramping fast and demand is insatiable, $1,000/share is in play for 2025. If he hedges on timing or mentions competitive pressures, the stock could revisit $700.

## The Bottom Line

Nvidia's Q4 earnings will either confirm the AI boom has legs for another 12-18 months or signal the beginning of a normalization cycle. With the stock up 220% over 12 months and trading at 35x forward earnings, expectations are sky-high. Wednesday's report isn't just about one quarter - it's a referendum on whether generative AI infrastructure spending sustains through 2025 or begins a necessary correction. Either way, expect volatility.
